Facts about Boxing

Boxing is a combat sport with ancient origins, dating back to ancient Greece, and is part of the modern Olympics since 1904. It is governed by rules, including the Queensberry Rules, which divide matches into rounds and dictate scoring by points, knockouts, or technical knockouts. Modern professional and amateur matches are often shorter and have more rounds compared to ancient contests.

History and evolution

Ancient origins: Fist-fighting contests have existed since prehistoric times, with the earliest visual evidence appearing in 3rd millennium BCE Sumerian relief carvings. It was an Olympic event in ancient Greece starting in 688 BCE. Modern rules: The modern version of the sport was established with the Queensberry Rules, which introduced gloves and timed rounds, a stark contrast to earlier bare-knuckle fighting. Olympic inclusion: Boxing has been a permanent fixture in the modern Summer Olympics since 1904, though it was temporarily banned in Sweden in 1912. Women's boxing was added to the Olympics starting in 2012.
